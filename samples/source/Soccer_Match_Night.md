Watched the Champions League match last night and it was absolutely worth staying up for. The game went into extra time, and the tension in those final twenty minutes was the kind that makes you forget to breathe. I caught myself leaning forward on the couch with every corner kick, fully convinced that posture alone would somehow help the ball go in.

What makes live football so different from other sports is how much of the game is nothing happening — and yet it never feels boring. The buildup, the shape-shifting of formations, the moment a midfielder pauses just a half-second longer than expected before threading a through ball. That pause carries the whole weight of a match.

The winning goal came from a counter that lasted maybe eight seconds. Eight seconds that made three hours of buildup completely worth it. Went to bed too late, woke up tired, and would do it again without hesitation.
