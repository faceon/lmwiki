I heavily refactored my backend directory today, ultimately deciding that Google Cloud Functions (GCF) is a better fit for my workflow than GitHub Actions. While using Gemini to assist with setting up Supabase tables and GCF logic, I hit a frustrating wall: the model consistently missed subtle nuances in the code.

There is a stark duality to AI right now. On a macro level, it has the potential to automate massive, labor-intensive industries—for instance, replacing a $1.5 billion transit labor market with a $300 million AI infrastructure. Yet, on a micro level, hands-on development still fundamentally requires a human developer to catch errors and correct the AI's contextual misunderstandings.
