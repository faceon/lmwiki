Started using Claude Code seriously today for the first time, and the experience was jarring in how different it felt from prompt-and-response workflows. The model reads files, runs commands, edits code, and commits—all on its own, without me steering each step. At one point I walked away to make coffee and came back to a refactored module I had been dreading for weeks.

What strikes me is the shift in my own role. I am no longer a typist translating ideas into code; I am more of a reviewer and decision-maker. The bottleneck has moved from implementation to judgment. Whether that is liberating or unsettling probably depends on the day.
